Building finishing materials

Home Service Building finishing materials

  • Toilets

    • Fixtures designed for the disposal of human waste. They come in various styles, including wall-mounted and floor-mounted, and can be equipped with flushing mechanisms for efficient waste removal.

  • Sinks

    • Also known as lavatories, these fixtures provide a basin for washing hands, dishes, and other items. They include kitchen sinks, bathroom sinks, and utility sinks, each tailored to specific functions and space requirements.

  • Showers

    • Fixtures designed for bathing, typically consisting of a showerhead, control valves, and a basin or tray. They can be part of a standalone unit or integrated into a bathtub setup.

  • Urinals

    • Fixtures specifically for urination, primarily found in public restrooms. They come in wall-mounted or floor-mounted styles and are designed to be space-efficient and reduce water usage compared to traditional toilets.

  • Bathtubs

    • Large fixtures intended for bathing, available in various shapes and sizes. They can be freestanding or built into the wall, with options for soaking or whirlpool features.

  • Flush Doors

    • Smooth, flat doors with minimal joints or crevices, making them easy to clean and maintain. Typically used in areas requiring high levels of hygiene, such as operating rooms and patient wards.

  • Impact-Resistant Doors

    • Built to withstand frequent use and impacts, these doors are made from durable materials like high-density polyethylene (HDPE) or fiberglass. They are ideal for high-traffic areas and help maintain cleanliness by resisting dents and scratches.

  • Automatic Doors

    • Doors that open and close automatically, reducing the need for physical contact and minimizing the spread of contaminants. They are commonly used in entrances and corridors to enhance accessibility and hygiene.

  • Swing Doors

    • Hinged doors that open in one direction, often equipped with smooth, non-porous surfaces and sealed edges to prevent the accumulation of dirt and bacteria. Suitable for areas where hands-free operation is beneficial.

  • Bi-Folding Doors

    • Space-saving doors that fold in on themselves, often used in smaller or confined spaces. They offer smooth, hygienic surfaces and are designed for ease of cleaning and maintenance.

  • Escalators

    • Escalators efficiently handle high volumes of foot traffic with continuous movement, adjustable speeds, and inclines. They are designed for durability and safety, incorporating features such as anti-slip steps, emergency stop buttons, and handrail sensors. Newer models also prioritize energy efficiency with variable speed drives, and their design can be customized to complement the building’s aesthetics and layout.

  • Elevators

    • Elevators provide essential vertical transportation in buildings with features that include customizable sizes and capacities, sophisticated control systems, and a focus on safety through emergency communication systems and overload sensors. Modern elevators emphasize energy efficiency with regenerative drives and LED lighting, and they can be tailored to match the building’s interior design with various finishes and aesthetic options.
